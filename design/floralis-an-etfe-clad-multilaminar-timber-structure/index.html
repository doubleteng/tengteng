Floralis: an ETFE-Clad, Multilaminar Timber Structure

Research · Timber · ETFE · Robotics/CNC · 2025
← Back to Research

Description

Floralis is a lightweight multilaminar timber prototype enclosed with ETFE foil, designed as a proof-of-concept shelter for Petrified Tree Stumps at Colorado’s Florissant Fossil Beds National Monument. The work advances reciprocal-diagram form-finding, design optimization, robotic and CNC fabrication strategies, and laser-survey-assisted assembly to achieve a precise, buildable funnel-shell lattice with an ETFE enclosure that maintains visitor visibility while mitigating environmental erosion.

Methods

A polyhedral graphic statics workflow informed the funnel-shell geometry and structural hierarchy. Milling data were automated from the PGS model into multi-axis CNC routines for sheet-timber laminae; articulated connectors and mitered aluminum channels supported the ETFE foils. Photogrammetry and laser surveying provided digital twins for on-site alignment and adjustments.
